# Finance Analyst — Project Evaluation

## About the Role

IronVale Resources is seeking a motivated Finance Analyst to join our Finance team and support the evaluation of our transformational Goldfields lithium project. This graduate-level position offers the opportunity to work directly on one of Western Australia's most significant critical minerals developments, contributing to financial modelling that will inform our final investment decision.

Reporting to Chief Financial Officer Tom Gallagher, you'll be part of a tight-knit finance team of six professionals supporting IronVale's evolution from a pure-play iron ore producer to a diversified critical minerals company. This role provides exceptional exposure to senior leadership and board-level decision making while developing core financial analysis skills in the dynamic resources sector.

## Key Responsibilities

- Develop and maintain comprehensive financial models for the Goldfields lithium project, incorporating capital expenditure, operating costs, and revenue projections
- Conduct sensitivity analysis and scenario planning to assess project risks and returns under various market conditions
- Support capital budgeting processes across IronVale's portfolio, comparing investment opportunities between iron ore optimisation and lithium development
- Prepare detailed financial presentations and briefing materials for executive leadership, board meetings, and potential investors
- Collaborate with our engineering and operations teams to validate cost assumptions and operational parameters
- Monitor lithium market trends and pricing forecasts to inform project economics
- Assist with quarterly financial reporting and variance analysis for existing operations
- Support due diligence activities and engage with external advisors including investment banks and independent engineers

## What You'll Bring

- Bachelor's degree in Finance, Accounting, Economics, or related field (graduated within the last 2 years)
- Advanced proficiency in Microsoft Excel, including financial modelling, scenario analysis, and data visualisation
- Understanding of discounted cash flow analysis, NPV/IRR calculations, and capital budgeting principles
- Strong analytical mindset with attention to detail and ability to work with large datasets
- Excellent written and verbal communication skills, with experience preparing executive-level presentations
- Interest in the resources sector and critical minerals value chain
- Ability to work collaboratively in a fast-paced environment while managing competing priorities
- Australian working rights and willingness to occasionally travel to project sites

*Preferred but not essential: Experience with financial software such as @RISK or Crystal Ball for Monte Carlo analysis, CFA Level 1 candidacy, or internship experience in resources, investment banking, or corporate finance.*

## What We Offer

- Competitive graduate salary package of $75,000 - $85,000 plus superannuation
- Comprehensive graduate development program with structured mentoring and rotation opportunities
- Direct exposure to major project development and M&A activities
- Professional development support including CFA study assistance and conference attendance
- Flexible working arrangements with hybrid work-from-home options
- Health and wellness benefits including private health insurance subsidy
- Opportunity to visit our operational sites and understand the full mining value chain
- Clear progression pathway to Senior Analyst and Finance Manager roles
- Participation in IronVale's equity incentive scheme after 12 months
