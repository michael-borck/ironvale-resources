# Health, Safety and Environment Policy

**Document Reference:** HSE-POL-001  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** 15 March 2024  
**Next Review Date:** 15 March 2026  
**Document Owner:** Brooke Lawson, Health, Safety & Emergency Manager  
**Approved By:** Karen Whitfield, Chief Operating Officer

## 1. Policy Statement

IronVale Resources is committed to maintaining the highest standards of health, safety, and environmental performance across all our operations. We recognise that our employees, contractors, communities, and the environment are fundamental to our long-term success and sustainability.

This policy applies to all IronVale Resources operations, including our Pilbara iron ore sites (Pilbara East and Pilbara Central), our Goldfields lithium project, Perth corporate headquarters, and all exploration activities. It encompasses traditional mining operations as well as our autonomous haulage systems and emerging technologies.

We are committed to:
- Achieving zero harm to people and the environment
- Continuous improvement in HSE performance
- Compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
- Transparent reporting of HSE performance to stakeholders

## 2. Leadership and Accountability

### 2.1 Executive Responsibility

The Chief Operating Officer, Karen Whitfield, has ultimate accountability for HSE performance across IronVale Resources. The General Manager — Sustainability & Environment, Dr. Ravi Mehta, provides strategic oversight of environmental compliance and sustainability initiatives.

Site Managers, including Pete Drummond at Pilbara East, are accountable for implementing this policy and ensuring daily HSE performance meets company standards. The Head of People & Community, Danielle Keenan, supports HSE culture development and workforce capability building.

### 2.2 Line Management Accountability

All supervisors and team leaders are responsible for:
- Implementing HSE procedures within their area of responsibility
- Ensuring workers under their supervision are competent and follow safe work practices
- Investigating incidents and near misses within their teams
- Promoting a positive HSE culture through visible leadership

### 2.3 Individual Responsibility

Every employee and contractor has the responsibility to:
- Work safely and protect their own health and that of others
- Follow all HSE procedures and use prescribed safety equipment
- Report hazards, incidents, and near misses immediately
- Participate in HSE training and competency development
- Exercise the right to stop work if conditions are unsafe

## 3. Safety Management System

### 3.1 Framework

IronVale Resources operates an integrated Safety Management System based on ISO 45001:2018 standards. Our system encompasses:
- Risk-based approach to safety management
- Systematic hazard identification and risk assessment
- Robust incident investigation and corrective action processes
- Regular safety audits and management reviews
- Continuous improvement through lessons learned

### 3.2 Critical Controls Management

We maintain a comprehensive Critical Controls Register covering major hazards including:
- Mobile equipment interactions (particularly relevant for autonomous haulage operations)
- Working at height
- Electrical hazards
- Confined spaces
- Ground instability
- Chemical and hazardous substance exposure

*Note: The Critical Controls Register for autonomous haulage is currently being updated following the Q2 2024 trial expansion. Expected completion December 2024.*

### 3.3 Training and Competency

All personnel must demonstrate competency before commencing work. This includes:
- Site-specific induction programs (duration varies by role: 2 days for contractors, 5 days for permanent employees)
- Task-specific training and assessment
- Regular refresher training (annually for high-risk activities)
- Leadership development programs for supervisors

Special provisions apply for autonomous haulage operations, requiring additional certification through our Technology Integration Training Program developed in partnership with equipment manufacturers.

## 4. Hazard Reporting and Risk Management

### 4.1 Hazard Identification

IronVale Resources employs multiple methods for hazard identification:
- Pre-shift risk assessments (mandatory for all work activities)
- Workplace inspections (weekly by supervisors, monthly by safety representatives)
- Hazard reporting through our SafetyFirst online portal
- Job safety analyses for non-routine tasks
- Change management processes for operational modifications

### 4.2 Reporting Requirements

All hazards, incidents, and near misses must be reported within 2 hours of identification. Reporting channels include:
- SafetyFirst online portal (preferred method)
- Direct notification to immediate supervisor
- 24-hour HSE hotline: 1800 IRONVALE (1800 476 682)
- Anonymous reporting through safety suggestion boxes

### 4.3 Risk Assessment Process

Risk assessments follow our standardised 5x5 matrix methodology:
- Consequence categories: Catastrophic, Major, Moderate, Minor, Insignificant
- Likelihood categories: Almost Certain, Likely, Possible, Unlikely, Rare
- Residual risk after controls must be As Low As Reasonably Practicable (ALARP)

High and extreme risks require approval from the Site Manager before work proceeds. Very high risks require COO approval and additional control measures.

## 5. Incident Investigation

### 5.1 Investigation Requirements

All incidents are investigated using the ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method) methodology. Investigation depth varies by incident severity:

**Level 1 (Minor):** Supervisor investigation, 48-hour completion
**Level 2 (Moderate):** Team investigation led by HSE representative, 5-day completion  
**Level 3 (Major/Potential Fatal):** Formal investigation team including external specialist, 21-day completion

### 5.2 Investigation Team Composition

Level 3 investigations include:
- Senior site representative (Site Manager or delegate)
- HSE professional (Brooke Lawson or nominee)
- Subject matter expert relevant to incident type
- Employee representative
- External specialist where required

### 5.3 Corrective Actions

All investigations must identify:
- Immediate causes (unsafe acts/conditions)
- Underlying causes (system failures, organisational factors)
- Root causes (management system deficiencies)

Corrective actions are tracked through our Action Management System with defined responsibility, completion dates, and effectiveness reviews.

## 6. Environmental Management

### 6.1 Environmental Compliance

Dr. Ravi Mehta oversees compliance with all environmental legislation including:
- Environmental Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (Cth)
- Mining Act 1978 (WA) and associated regulations
- Environmental Protection Act 1986 (WA)
- Native Title Act 1993 (Cth)

### 6.2 Environmental Monitoring

Our environmental monitoring programs cover:
- Air quality (dust, particulates, noise levels)
- Water quality (groundwater, surface water, discharge quality)
- Flora and fauna monitoring (quarterly surveys at all sites)
- Cultural heritage protection in consultation with Traditional Owners
- Waste management and recycling performance

### 6.3 Rehabilitation and Closure

Progressive rehabilitation occurs across all sites with:
- Annual rehabilitation targets (minimum 15 hectares per site)
- Native species revegetation programs
- Ongoing monitoring for minimum 3 years post-rehabilitation
- Community engagement in closure planning (particularly relevant for Goldfields lithium project)

*Note: Updated rehabilitation standards for lithium operations are under development following recent regulatory changes. Expected implementation Q1 2025.*

## 7. Emergency Response

### 7.1 Emergency Response Capability

Each site maintains emergency response capabilities including:
- Trained emergency response teams (minimum 8 members per shift)
- Emergency response equipment and vehicles
- Medical facilities staffed by qualified personnel
- Evacuation procedures for different emergency scenarios
- Regular emergency drills (quarterly for major emergencies)

### 7.2 Business Continuity

Emergency response plans address both immediate safety concerns and business continuity, particularly important given our FIFO workforce model and remote site locations.

### 7.3 Community Emergency Response

Sites maintain coordination protocols with local emergency services and provide emergency response assistance to nearby communities where appropriate, consistent with our Community Partnership Agreements.

## 8. Continuous Improvement

### 8.1 Performance Monitoring

HSE performance is monitored through:
- Leading indicators: safety observations, near miss reporting rates, training completion
- Lagging indicators: injury rates, environmental exceedances, regulatory penalties
- Monthly HSE reporting to Executive Team
- Quarterly Board HSE Committee reporting

### 8.2 Management Review

Formal management reviews occur annually, incorporating:
- HSE performance against targets
- Regulatory compliance status
- Effectiveness of management systems
- Resource allocation and capability requirements
- Lessons learned from incidents and industry best practices

### 8.3 External Benchmarking

We participate in industry HSE benchmarking through:
- Minerals Council of Australia safety performance reporting
- Chamber of Minerals and Energy WA safety statistics
- International Council on Mining and Metals (ICMM) reporting principles

## 9. Implementation and Communication

This policy is communicated through:
- Mandatory HSE inductions for all personnel
- Regular toolbox talks and safety meetings
- Annual HSE performance reporting
- IronVale Resources intranet and notice boards
- Contractor pre-qualification and onboarding processes
